What I learned is that while I knew Jupiter has an effect on the solar system like how it redirects asteroids from hitting the Earth what I didn't know is how pivotal the mere presence of Jupiter was that changed our entire solar system. When gas giants like Jupiter first emerged in early solar systems they typically formed close to their host star and then migrate outwards this was likely the case with Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. But Jupiter is thought to be responsible for many more things such as causing two terrestrial planets to smash into each other thus creating the Earth and the Moon, disrupting the gravitational influence of the debris and the asteroid belts resulting in the absence of any planetary formation and the reduction of planetary Mass available to Mars during its early stages of formation resulting in the smaller planet that we see today.
